Frequently Asked Questions

What are the signs of tomato blight?

Tomato blight can cause yellowing and browning of leaves, as well as dark spots on the fruit. The stems may also turn brown and the plant may become stunted. If left untreated, tomato blight can cause the plant to die.

How do I control aphids on my plants?

Aphids can be controlled with insecticidal soap or neem oil. You can also try spraying the plants with a strong stream of water to knock the aphids off. Ladybugs and lacewings are natural predators of aphids and can help keep their populations in check.

What is the best way to get rid of nematodes in the soil?

Nematodes can be controlled with crop rotation, cover cropping, and using soil solarization. You can also try applying beneficial nematodes, which are natural predators of harmful nematodes. In some cases, it may be necessary to cover the soil with clear or black plastic for several weeks during the hottest part of the year to kill off nematodes.

What is the best way to control fungal diseases on my plants?

Fungal diseases can be controlled by maintaining good air circulation around plants, avoiding overhead watering, and removing infected plant material. You can also try using fungicides or homemade remedies such as a baking soda spray or a milk spray.

How can I prevent weeds from taking over my garden?

Weeds can be prevented by using mulch, planting cover crops, and practicing good weed management techniques such as hand weeding and hoeing. You can also try using herbicides, but be sure to read and follow the instructions carefully to avoid harming your plants.
